# Copywriting — Simple Summary

## Why Great Content Matters

High-quality content is one of the pillars of a successful SEO strategy. Google's mission is to organize the world's information and surface the best result — so if your content isn't great, it's hard to be that result. Good content also helps you stand out in competitive markets and simply provides a better service to your readers.

## Positive Effects on SEO

Writing well pays off in several ways:
1. **Your message comes across better** — for readers *and* for Google. Clear, well-structured writing helps both understand your point, which can boost rankings.
2. **Lower bounce rate** — well-written, well-structured content keeps people on your site instead of leaving immediately, which helps rankings.
3. **More trust** — clean, typo-free, professional writing makes readers trust you (and come back, or buy).
4. **More social media attention** — content has to be good before anyone bothers sharing it.

## The 3 Elements of Great Content

Once you've done your keyword research, high-quality, SEO-friendly content needs to be:
1. **Original** — fresh and different from what's already out there. Note: original doesn't have to mean brand-new information — a personal take or a well-structured overview of existing info also counts.
2. **Readable** — clear and well-structured, so both people *and* Google can grasp your message easily.
3. **Findable** — optimized so Google can actually pick it up in search — but findability should never come at the cost of originality or readability. This is where Yoast SEO helps fine-tune things.

## What Makes a Text Readable?

**Structure:**
- Intro → body → conclusion.
- Paragraphs broken up by clear subheadings.
- Each paragraph opens with a strong **topic sentence** that previews what it's about.

**Style:**
- Short, clear sentences over long, convoluted ones.
- Use transition words (*"in contrast," "additionally," "therefore"*) to connect ideas.
- Avoid unnecessarily complicated words when simpler ones work just as well.

## Why Readability Is Important

- **For your audience** — people want a pleasant, easy reading experience.
- **For scannability** — people scan before committing to read fully; subheadings and topic sentences let them quickly judge if your content answers their question.
- **For Google's algorithm** — Google uses structural signals (like headings) to understand what a page is about, similar to how humans read.
- **For voice search** — spoken search results need to be clear and digestible, since nobody wants to listen to a long, convoluted sentence read aloud.

## The Yoast SEO Readability Analysis

Yoast SEO includes a readability checker (in the meta box/sidebar) that scores your text and gives actionable feedback. It checks for:

- **Transition words** — are you using them to keep the text coherent?
- **Consecutive sentences** — do too many sentences in a row start with the same word? (repetitive and annoying)
- **Paragraph length** — overly long paragraphs look daunting; break them up.
- **Subheading distribution** — enough subheadings to make the text scannable?
- **Sentence length** — sentences over ~20 words become hard to follow; keep them short.
- **Passive voice** — overusing passive voice ("the SEO score is calculated by Yoast SEO") makes text distant and harder to parse than active voice ("Yoast SEO calculates your SEO score").
- **Text alignment (Premium)** — flags center-aligned blocks over 50 characters, since centered text is harder to read; left-aligned (or right-aligned for RTL languages) is recommended.
- **Word complexity (Premium)** — flags text where over 10% of words are "complex," suggesting simpler alternatives.

## Key Takeaways

- High-quality content is essential for SEO success.
- Great content must be **original, readable, and findable**.
- Readability matters both for your human readers and for your Google ranking.
- The Yoast SEO readability analysis is a built-in tool to help you write clearer, more effective content.
