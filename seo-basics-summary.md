# How Search Engines Work — Simple Summary

## Search Engines You Should Know About
Google dominates globally (~90% market share), but others matter depending on region: Bing and Yahoo have a notable share in the US, Yandex leads in Russia, and Baidu leads in China. Privacy-focused engines like DuckDuckGo and Startpage exist too, plus Ecosia, which uses its profits to plant trees. Courses focus on Google since it's the biggest and most influential.

## How Search Engines Actually Work — 3 Parts
1. **Spiders (crawlers/bots)** – little programs that constantly roam the web following links, collecting text, images, videos, etc.
2. **The index** – a giant database where spiders save a copy of every page they find. It updates whenever your site changes.
3. **The algorithm** – decides which indexed pages to show and in what order, based on a user's search query. Nobody knows the exact ranking factors, and they change often.

## How Search Engines Find Your Site
Your site gets discovered when another already-known site links to it — that's how the first crawl happens. You can't just "submit" your site anymore. Links also signal *importance*: more (and higher-quality) links pointing to a page = more weight. Good "crawlability" (nothing blocking the bots) is essential, or your pages won't get indexed.

## RankBrain (Advanced)
A machine-learning system that helps Google understand the *meaning* behind search terms, not just keywords — and improves itself over time.

## Key Google Updates Over the Years
- **Panda (2011):** penalized thin content and low-value affiliate sites.
- **Penguin (2012):** cracked down on fake/bought links.
- **Hummingbird (2013):** understood full queries (not just isolated words) — paved the way for voice search.
- **Mobilegeddon (2015):** boosted mobile-friendly sites in mobile search.
- **Possum (2016):** made local results vary more by searcher location and query phrasing.
- **Mobile-first index (2018):** ranks sites based on their *mobile* version, not desktop.
- **Medic/Query Intent Update (2018):** better matches content to the true intent behind a question.
- **Continuous updates (2018–now):** Google now tweaks its algorithm constantly, making it hard to "game" — best strategy is just making your site genuinely useful.
- **BERT (2019):** improved Google's language understanding, reading context in both directions instead of just one.

## Bottom Line
Google is the search engine that matters most, it finds and ranks sites through crawling → indexing → algorithm, and its ranking rules keep evolving — so the safest long-term SEO strategy is focusing on genuine usefulness for people rather than chasing every update.
